What to Expect

This is a legally binding process, and there are several steps involved, but there are people and resources that can help guide you through each part of the process.

Plan to spend 2-3 hours in court when you go to file your protection order. If you have children, try to arrange childcare so that you will have the time and emotional energy you need to complete the paperwork

Step-by-Step

  • 1. Obtaining and completing the forms

    Once you complete the forms you will need to file them with the Clerk’s Office. The clerk will stamp a case number on your forms and will then send you to see a Judge or Commissioner who will decide if a two week, temporary order should be granted. This is referred to as the ex parte hearing.

  • 2. Filing the forms with the Court

    Once you complete the forms you will need to file them with the Clerk’s Office. The clerk will stamp a case number on your forms and will then send you to see a Judge or Commissioner who will decide if a two week, temporary order should be granted. This is referred to as the ex parte hearing.

  • 3. Presenting your order to a Judge/Commissioner

    Once you complete the forms you will need to file them with the Clerk’s Office. The clerk will stamp a case number on your forms and will then send you to see a Judge or Commissioner who will decide if a two week, temporary order should be granted. This is referred to as the ex parte hearing.

  • 4. Filing your signed order with the Clerk’s Office

    Once you complete the forms you will need to file them with the Clerk’s Office. The clerk will stamp a case number on your forms and will then send you to see a Judge or Commissioner who will decide if a two week, temporary order should be granted. This is referred to as the ex parte hearing.

Where Do I file?

You can file in District Court or in Superior Court. (There is no legal difference.)

You can file in District Court

King County District Court address | directions

If you file in District Court your documents will need to be filed with the District Court Clerk by 10:30am to be seen by the judge that day. Otherwise you will be directed to return the following day.

You can file in Superior Court

King County Superior Court address | directions

In Superior Court you will have until 3:30pm to be seen by an ex-parte commissioner.
